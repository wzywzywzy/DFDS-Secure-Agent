# Matej's 30s slot — "Future improvements"

Position: last content slide before the close. Tone is forward-
looking and confident — "we have a plan, the architecture extends
cleanly, here's where it goes."

The 4 bullets on the right are fixed. The script below is tuned for
~30 seconds and naturally bridges from Zuck's summary slot.

---

## Recommended script (≈80 words ≈ 28s)

> "Building on what Zuck just showed — here's what's next.
>
> **One** — real-time alerts for high-risk emails. Operators see
> them on arrival, not at the bottom of a queue.
>
> **Two** — a third decision button: *'ask the customer for more
> info'*. Some emails are genuinely neither approve nor reject.
>
> **Three** — longer context windows, so we reason about full
> email threads instead of one message at a time.
>
> **Four** — Kong Observability plugins, turning the whole
> pipeline into a production-ready telemetry surface.
>
> **Same architecture, broader coverage. Thanks!**"

---

## Delivery notes

- **"Building on what Zuck just showed"** — flick a hand toward
  Zuck. One-second handoff, audience tracks the speaker.
- Number each bullet aloud (**One ... Two ... Three ... Four**).
  It gives the audience a rhythm and tells them when you're
  almost done.
- The phrase *"genuinely neither approve nor reject"* is the
  human-system insight — emphasise it. It's the most original of
  the four bullets; the others are scaling work.
- **"Same architecture, broader coverage"** — slow, almost casual.
  This is the closing line. It tells the panel: *we didn't
  oversell, what we shipped is structurally sound*.
- End on **"Thanks!"** with eye contact at the head judge. Don't
  walk off-stage on a bullet point.

## If you want to lead from the "Today" left column

Use this version if the slide has the Today/Next two-column layout:

> "Today we shipped four pillars — authorization, redaction,
> auditability, human-in-the-loop. **Here's what production would
> add.**
>
> Real-time alerts for high-risk emails — operators see them on
> arrival.
>
> A third decision button — 'ask the customer for more info' —
> for the emails that are genuinely neither approve nor reject.
>
> Longer context windows for full email threads.
>
> Kong Observability for production telemetry.
>
> **Thanks!**"

≈ 75 words ≈ 27s. Slightly tighter; works only if the left column
is on the slide so the audience can see the four pillars while
you're listing them.

---

## Even shorter version (≈50 words ≈ 18-20s)

If your team runs over time and you need to cut Matej's slot:

> "Four next steps. **One** — real-time alerts for high-risk
> emails. **Two** — an 'ask for more info' decision button.
> **Three** — multi-message thread context. **Four** — Kong
> Observability for production telemetry. Same architecture,
> broader coverage. Thanks!"

Cut the *"Building on what Zuck showed"* opener and the gloss on
each bullet. Lands the same content in 18-20s.

---

## What NOT to say

- **Don't apologise** for what's missing. *"We didn't have time
  for X"* turns the panel from "they have a plan" to "they ran
  out of time". Frame everything as a forward step.
- **Don't list more than 4 items.** Four is already at the limit
  of what an audience can hold; adding "and also..." dilutes
  every prior bullet.
- **Don't get specific about implementation** — *"we'd use
  Prometheus..."* — at 30s every word competes. The audience
  trusts you, they just want to see the shape.
- **Don't end on Kong Observability** alone. End on *"same
  architecture, broader coverage"* — that line frames every
  bullet you just listed as **a natural extension**, not gaps.
